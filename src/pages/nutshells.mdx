## : component: ysws

<Emoji name="shipitparrot" /> At Hack Club, we believe the best way to learn is to build & ship real projects - that's why we run a huge variety of "You Ship, We Ship" (YSWS) programs & events throughout the year. Most of these programs follow a similar format: you build something awesome, ship it to the world, and we'll physically ship you some cool stuff to help you keep building! Previous YSWS programs have granted domain credits, drawing robots, microcontrollers, stickers, t-shirts, and more.

Check out the current YSWS programs at [ysws.hackclub.com](https://ysws.hackclub.com). An awesome starter YSWS is [Boba Drops](https://boba.hackclub.com) - build your first website and get $5 to spend on boba!

## : component: programs

<Emoji name="summer25"/> Throughout the year, Hack Club HQ runs multiple large-scale virtual events to help you learn new skills & get awesome stuff to help you build your projects. We usually run a summer and winter "general YSWS" - where you can build anything you want, ship it to get a virtual currency, and spend that currency on things like laptops, hosting credits, swag, 3D printers, and more. Past examples include [Summer of Making](https://summer.hackclub.com) (summer 2025), and [High Seas](https://highseas.hackclub.com) (winter 2024).

We've also run more specialized events like [Highway](https://highway.hackclub.com) (build hardware projects, get them funded), [Milkyway](https://milkyway.hackclub.com) (code games, get prizes), and [Siege](https://siege.hackclub.com) (ship something every week for 10 weeks, get a Framework laptop).

## : component: hq events

<Emoji name="hackers-assemble" /> Hack Club runs multiple IRL events throughout the year. Over time we've [hosted 150 teens for a hardware hackathon at GitHub HQ](https://undercity.hackclub.com), [built a hacker cafe in Shanghai](https://juice.hackclub.com), [turned a Vermont campground into a hacker oasis](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=O1s5HqSqKi0), and so, so much more. These adventures are special - you'll build amazing technical projects while making lifelong friends in amazing places around the world.

Hack Club events are usually merit-based, meaning that the only condition to attend is building awesome projects and submitting them to the program! Your experience will be fully funded, including lodging, food, local transit, etc. Flight stipends (possibly need-based ones as well) are often available too.

## component: satellites

<Emoji name="daydream"/> A few times a year, Hack Clubbers across the world come together to host "satellite hackathon" events - hundreds of local, teen-organized, in-person hackathons happening simultaneously worldwide. These events are a great way to meet other local hackers, build cool projects, and have fun with friends! Past examples include [Daydream](https://daydream.hackclub.com) (simultaneous game jams in 100+ cities), and [Scrapyard](https://scrapyard.hackclub.com) (a hackathon for stupid ideas, in 50+ cities). Community members also frequently organize their own local hackathons - check out [hackathons.hackclub.com](https://hackathons.hackclub.com) to find one near you!

Satellite hackathon organizers get access to funding, swag, mentorship from Hack Club HQ, and a supportive community of other teen organizers around the world.

## : component: hcb

<Emoji name="hcbparty"/> HCB is the foundation for your nonprofit. Apply to create a nonprofit fund in minutes, then raise money with US 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status, automatic taxes & accounting, pre-made donation pages, and access to perks like a free domain and Google Workspace. Issue physical and digital cards to spend your nonprofit dollars, reimburse via check, ACH, and more, and optionally make your finances public to build trust with your community.

HCB has been used to raise more than $67 million for projects that make the world a better place. No minimum balance, no credit card fees, just a simple 7% fee on income to cover operational costs. [Apply now.](https://nonprofit.new)

## : component: internship

<Emoji name="sos-orpheus-yay" /> Teenagers that have significantly contributed to Hack Club and its community may be invited to join the Hack Club team in Shelburne, Vermont as a summer intern or gap year fellow. As an intern or gappie, you'll work closely with HQ staff to build and maintain programs, events, and resources for the Hack Club community. These are paid, full time positions, and you'll get to live and work with some of the most talented and passionate teen hackers in the world.

Internships are usually 8-12 weeks long during the summer, while gap years are for teens that want to take a year off between high school and college to work full time at Hack Club HQ.

## : wait, what's a "hacker?"

good question! contrary to popular belief, a "hacker" isn't someone who breaks into computer systems or does anything illegal. a hacker is simply someone who solves problems in creative ways - usually with code. hackers build, iterate, break, and ship things that make the world better. if you're someone who likes to tinker, create, and learn new things, then congratulations: you're a hacker!
